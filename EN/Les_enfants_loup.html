Dicta

A film that moves me

The wolf children is a film that was directed by Mamoru Hosada in 2012, it is a Japanese animation film that speaks of family and the difficulties to integrate when you are different. It is a film that in my opinion is sad and has a beautiful moral in any case what I remember is that we must accept the differences that are ours and that of others.

Escape from the movies

I think this movie had a positive impact on the person I am now because I have a great open mind and I often felt different when I was little but I always accepted it even if it was difficult with the others. This movie also allowed me to discover the world of animated films and anime which are an integral part of my current universe, I hope that in my future life it will always be the case because it is an important movie for me.Films, like books and music, escape from this world but not only. Movies are part of our culture, they make us think and give us good morals, they give us another vision. Each character is developed in such a way that we come to represent ourselves in them, the scenes when the three main characters are together are striking and full of emotion, we want to enter the film and live with them.

The representation in one of the characters

One of the characters that marked me the most is Ame, the youngest son of Yuri. He was born like his older sister, being half human, half wolf. Unlike his sister who wants to banish her wolf part from her life, his brother Ame wants to live as a wolf and doesn't want to face the children of his age. When he takes his human form, he tries to avoid people other than his family, he is shy and hardly speaks, usually he runs away and goes into the forest to be alone. This is the character I pictured most when I was little and even now
